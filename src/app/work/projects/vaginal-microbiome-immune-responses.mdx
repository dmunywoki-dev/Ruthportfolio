## Overview

This research, currently under review for publication, investigates how different compositions of the vaginal microbiome influence local immune system activity. Specifically, it examines the association between a state of non-Lactobacillus dominance and the expression of cervicovaginal cytokine receptors.

## Research Focus

- **Microbiome States:** Characterizing different community state types of the vaginal microbiome.
- **Immune Receptor Expression:** Quantifying the expression of key cytokine receptors in the cervicovaginal environment.
- **Host-Microbe Interaction:** Analyzing the statistical association between microbiome composition and immune responses.

## Technologies Used

- **Methodology:** Bioinformatics analysis, mathematical modeling, and statistical correlation.
- **Lab Techniques:** Utilized data from PCR, ELISA, and sequencing to inform the computational models.

## Outcome

The findings suggest that a non-Lactobacillus dominant microbiome is linked to altered immune surveillance in the female genital tract. This work provides a deeper understanding of the mechanisms through which the microbiome may contribute to susceptibility to infections and other adverse health outcomes.
