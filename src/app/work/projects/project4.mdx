## Overview

This research evaluated the relationship between the cervicovaginal microbiome and host immune responses. Specifically, we analyzed how non-Lactobacillus dominance correlates with changes in cytokine receptor expression, which has significant implications for susceptibility to infectious diseases.

## Key Highlights

- **Data Analysis:** Performed statistical modeling and epidemiological data analysis on clinical samples.
- **Bioinformatics:** Utilized sequencing and bioinformatic pipelines to map microbiome diversity.
- **Publication:** The findings are currently under review, contributing crucial knowledge to the field of host-pathogen interactions.
