## Overview

This ongoing research project focuses on understanding how the vaginal microbiome contributes to the acquisition and persistence of Human Papillomavirus (HPV). By integrating advanced statistical techniques with biological data, the goal is to map the specific pathways through which microbiome dysbiosis heightens susceptibility to infection.

## Key Features

- **Mathematical Modeling:** Developing rigorous models that simulate the progression of HPV infection based on host microbiome composition.
- **Data Cleaning & Management:** Establishing automated bioinformatic workflows for organizing large-scale infectious disease datasets.
- **Statistical Analysis:** Utilizing predictive clustering and regression models to uncover hidden patterns in host-pathogen interactions.

## Technologies Used

- **Tools:** R, SPSS, Python
- **Techniques:** Descriptive and Inferential Statistics, Mathematical Modeling, Genomic Data Analysis

## Outcome

This study contributes to high-impact scientific manuscripts and grant submissions. The resulting models are expected to significantly advance our understanding of microbial drivers of cervical cancer risk, ultimately guiding better public health interventions.
