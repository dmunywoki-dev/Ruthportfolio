## Overview

Conducted within the Mishra Lab at St. Michael's Hospital, Toronto, this project involved the rigorous cleaning and analysis of large-scale HIV self-testing datasets collected from men who have sex with men (MSM) in Kenya.

## Key Features

- **Data Cleaning:** Extracted and pre-processed raw field data, transforming it into analyzable epidemiological formats.
- **Regression Modeling:** Designed and deployed regression models to understand behavioral predictors for HIV testing.
- **Model Validation:** Validated predictive models, checking for accuracy and standardizing output errors.
- **Clustering Methods:** Mapped demographic and geographic clusters relevant to test adoption and incidence rates.

## Technologies Used

- **Data Science Tools:** R, SPSS  
- **Techniques:** Data imputation, clustering, model validation, cohort studies

## Outcome

The findings directly informed the development of a targeted screening tool, ensuring undiagnosed HIV cases could be better addressed and guided public health policy adjustments regarding self-testing resources.
