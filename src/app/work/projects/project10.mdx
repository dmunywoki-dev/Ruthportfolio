## Research Overview

This ongoing postdoctoral research at McGill University focuses on understanding the intricate relationship between the microbiome and human papillomavirus (HPV). By applying advanced statistical modeling and bioinformatics analysis, the project aims to predict microbiome contributions to HPV acquisition and persistence.

## Key Responsibilities

- **Statistical Modeling:** Developing mathematical models for infectious disease studies.
- **Bioinformatics:** Analyzing complex genomic and microbiome data.
- **Mentorship:** Mentoring junior researchers and guiding them through research methodologies.
- **Grant & Publication Writing:** Preparing manuscripts, presentations, and grant submissions to secure funding and share findings.

## Tools & Techniques

- **Data Analysis:** R, SPSS, Regression, Clustering, Longitudinal analysis
- **Bioinformatics:** Genomic data analysis workflows
- **Domain Expertise:** Epidemiology, Infectious Disease Research

## Desired Outcome

To generate high-impact scientific publications and uncover actionable insights that could inform future preventive or therapeutic strategies against HPV infections.
