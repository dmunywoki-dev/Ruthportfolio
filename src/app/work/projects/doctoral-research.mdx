## Overview

My doctoral research focused on the intricate interplay between the host immune system and pathogens in the context of infectious diseases. This comprehensive work combined wet-lab experiments with advanced computational analysis to generate novel insights.

## Key Features

- **Wet-Lab Experimentation:** Performed a range of molecular biology techniques, including PCR, ELISA, sequencing, and tissue imaging.
- **Data Management:** Managed and processed large-scale epidemiological datasets using R and SPSS.
- **Bioinformatics:** Developed and implemented bioinformatic workflows for genomic and proteomic data analysis.
- **Statistical Analysis:** Conducted descriptive and inferential statistical analyses to identify significant immune correlates and interactions.

## Technologies Used

- **Lab Techniques:** PCR, ELISA, Next-Generation Sequencing, Tissue Imaging
- **Software & Tools:** R, SPSS, Python, GraphPad Prism

## Outcome

This research contributed to a deeper understanding of immune mechanisms in infectious diseases, leading to multiple publications and presentations. The findings have implications for developing new diagnostic markers and therapeutic strategies.
